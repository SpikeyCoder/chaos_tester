What an AI website audit checks

  • AI visibility: whether ChatGPT and other assistants can describe your business correctly and recommend it for the things you do.
  • Crawlability: whether AI crawlers can read your pages, or whether a JavaScript-only shell hides your content from them.
  • Structured data: whether your business name, services, and contact details are marked up in a way machines can parse.
  • Security and performance: headers, broken links, and load speed, since a slow or insecure page weakens how often you get surfaced.

How the check works

You enter a URL and the audit fetches your site the way an AI crawler would. It reads your content, inspects your structured data and metadata, and then asks live AI models how they describe and rank your business for queries a customer might use. The result is a plain-language report: where you show up, where you do not, and the specific changes that move the needle.

Why AI visibility matters now

Search is shifting from a list of links to a single answer. When someone asks an assistant "who is the best plumber near me" or "what tool audits a website," the model names a few options and skips the rest. If your site is hard for a model to read or your information is thin, you are invisible at the exact moment a customer is deciding. An AI website audit measures that gap before it costs you the lead.
